# Pipe Robot Safety Checklist

## Before Testing

- Confirm the pipe is owned or controlled by you and is safe to access
- Use clean water for early tests
- Keep all exposed test electronics out of water
- Use low voltage power and current limiting
- Pressure test sealed pods before installing expensive electronics
- Add a physical tether that can retrieve the robot without powered drive
- Add an operator-accessible emergency stop for powered bench tests

## During Testing

- Keep hands clear of tracks and gears
- Never seal damaged batteries inside the robot
- Stop immediately if the enclosure fogs, leaks, smells hot, or behaves oddly
- Keep the robot within tether reach during early water tests
- Log failures and dry the robot before inspecting electronics

## After Testing

- Disconnect power before opening the enclosure
- Dry and inspect all seals
- Clean and sanitize the chassis when testing outside a clean lab pipe
- Check the tether, strain relief, and retrieval eyelet for wear
- Review logs before the next run

## Not Yet Ready For

- Potable water systems
- Wastewater lines
- Pressurized pipes
- Confined-space entry scenarios
- Public infrastructure without written permission and a retrieval plan
